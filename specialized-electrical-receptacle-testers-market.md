Analog receptacle testers use a needle or LED display to indicate the presence of voltage and the specific wiring issues. These testers are simple and economical but lack accuracy. On the other hand, digital receptacle testers use an LCD screen to display comprehensive information about voltage, wiring issues, and grounding problems.
