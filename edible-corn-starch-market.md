<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The edible corn starch market consists of two types: Amylose corn starch and Amylopectin corn starch. Amylose corn starch is made up of long chains of glucose molecules, which gives it a higher gelatinization temperature and a more resistant structure. It is commonly used in food products that require a good texture stability, such as baked goods and snacks. On the other hand, Amylopectin corn starch is composed of highly branched glucose chains, making it more easily digestible. It is often used in products that require a smooth and creamy texture, like soups, sauces, and desserts.</p></p>
