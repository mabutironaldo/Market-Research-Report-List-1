<p><p>A petrol (gasoline) engine is a type of internal combustion engine that operates on the principle of converting chemical energy from gasoline into mechanical energy. It is commonly used in vehicles such as cars, motorcycles, and small to medium-sized boats. The resulting force from these explosions is used to generate power, which is then transferred to drive the wheels or propeller.</p><p>The future outlook of the petrol engine market remains promising, albeit with some challenges on the horizon. <p><p>The petrol (gasoline) engine market consists of two types: 2-stroke and 4-stroke engines. A 2-stroke engine completes its combustion cycle in two strokes of the piston, producing power every revolution. It has a simple design, compact size, and higher power output but emits more pollutants and has lower fuel efficiency. On the other hand, a 4-stroke engine completes its combustion cycle in four strokes, offering better fuel efficiency, lower emissions, and smoother operation. However, it is larger, more complex, and generates power every two engine revolutions.</p></p>
