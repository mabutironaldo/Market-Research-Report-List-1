<p><p>The pharmaceutical-grade cholesterol market is classified into two types: NF Grade and BP Grade. NF Grade refers to cholesterol that complies with the standards set by the National Formulary (NF), a book of public standards for medicines in the United States. It is suitable for pharmaceutical purposes and meets the necessary quality and purity requirements. On the other hand, BP Grade cholesterol meets the standards outlined by the British Pharmacopoeia (BP), a regulatory authority for pharmaceutical products in the United Kingdom. It signifies the cholesterol's adherence to quality, safety, and efficacy standards for pharmaceutical use. Both NF and BP Grades ensure the cholesterol is of high quality and suitable for medicinal applications.</p></p>
