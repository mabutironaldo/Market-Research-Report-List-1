<p><ul><li>Inorganic Concrete Corrosion Inhibitors</li><li>Organic Concrete Corrosion Inhibitors</li><li>Polymer Concrete Corrosion Inhibitors</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Concrete corrosion inhibitors are substances added to concrete mixtures to prevent or slow down the corrosion process. Inorganic concrete corrosion inhibitors are typically metallic compounds that form a protective layer on the surface, while organic concrete corrosion inhibitors are organic compounds that inhibit the corrosion process by adsorbing onto the metal surface. Polymer concrete corrosion inhibitors consist of polymers that chemically react with the concrete to form a protective layer. All three types of inhibitors play a crucial role in protecting concrete structures from corrosion caused by exposure to harsh environments.</p></p>
