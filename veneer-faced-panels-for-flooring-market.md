<p><p>Veneer-faced panels for flooring are available in two main market types: particleboard-faced and MDF-faced. Particleboard-faced veneer panels consist of a thin layer of veneer glued onto a particleboard core, providing a cost-effective option with moderate durability. MDF-faced veneer panels, on the other hand, have a medium-density fiberboard core coated with a thin layer of veneer. This market type offers higher durability, stability, and a smoother surface, making it a preferable choice for high traffic areas. Both options provide a visually appealing, wood-like finish to flooring surfaces.</p></p>
