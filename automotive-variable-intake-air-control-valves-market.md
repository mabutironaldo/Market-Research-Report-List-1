<p><p>The automotive variable intake air control valves market consists of two main types: solenoid valves and air operated valves. Solenoid valves are electrically controlled valves that use a solenoid to open and close the valve. They are commonly used in engine management systems to regulate the flow of intake air. On the other hand, air operated valves utilize compressed air or gas to operate the valve. These valves are often used in applications where high-speed operation or large flow capacities are required, such as turbocharger systems.</p></p>
