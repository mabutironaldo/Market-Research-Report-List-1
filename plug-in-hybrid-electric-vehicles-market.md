<p><p>Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs) come in two market types based on their power sources - the stored electricity market and the on-board electric generator market. In the stored electricity market, PHEVs use a rechargeable battery that can be charged by plugging into an outlet. This battery powers the electric motor, allowing the vehicle to operate solely on electricity. In the on-board electric generator market, PHEVs have an internal combustion engine that acts as a generator, supplying electricity to the motor and charging the battery while driving. This ensures a continuous power supply and eliminates the need to charge from an external source.</p></p>
