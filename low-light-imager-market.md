<p><p>The Low Light Imager market can be divided into two primary types: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor). CCD technology is known for its high image quality, sensitivity, and low noise levels, making it suitable for applications like surveillance cameras and scientific imaging. CMOS technology, on the other hand, offers lower power consumption, higher integration capabilities, and better cost-effectiveness, making it more popular in consumer electronics and mobile devices. Both CCD and CMOS image sensors play a crucial role in capturing images in low-light conditions, but they differ in terms of performance and application areas.</p></p>
