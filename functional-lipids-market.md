<p><p>Functional lipids are classified into two main types: plant source and animal source. Plant source functional lipids are derived from plants such as soybeans, corn, sunflower, and flaxseed. These lipids contain essential fatty acids, phytosterols, and other functional components that offer health benefits like improving heart health and reducing inflammation. Animal source functional lipids, on the other hand, are derived from sources like fish, eggs, and milk. These lipids are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, conjugated linoleic acid (CLA), and other bioactive compounds that contribute to various health benefits including brain development and immune system support.</p></p>
