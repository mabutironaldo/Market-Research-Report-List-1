<p><p>Phenolic wood bonding adhesives are used for joining wood materials together. Granular resin is a type of adhesive that comes in the form of small granules or pellets. It is commonly used in hot melt applications, where the resin is melted and applied onto the wood surfaces for bonding. Flaky resin, on the other hand, is a type of adhesive that is in the form of thin flakes. It is typically mixed with a liquid catalyst before application and is commonly used for cold press bonding of wood materials.</p></p>
