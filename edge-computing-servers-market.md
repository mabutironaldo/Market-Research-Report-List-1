<p><p>Edge computing servers are categorized into several types based on their form factor and architecture. Blade servers are compact units that can be easily stacked and provide high-density computing capabilities. Rack servers are larger and ideal for data centers, offering scalability and simplified cable management. Hyper-converged infrastructure (HCI) combines compute, storage, and networking into a single integrated system, reducing complexity and enhancing efficiency. Lastly, the other market includes servers that do not fit into these specific categories, such as modular servers or customized solutions, catering to specific edge computing requirements.</p></p>
