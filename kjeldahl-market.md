<p><p>Kjeldahl is a widely used method for determining the nitrogen content in organic substances, typically used in the food and beverage industry to measure the protein content of food products. The method involves digestion of a sample with sulfuric acid, which converts the organic nitrogen into ammonium sulfate. The ammonium sulfate is then distilled and the ammonia is collected in a boric acid solution, resulting in the formation of ammonium borate. The nitrogen present in the sample is determined by titration of the ammonium borate solution.</p><p>The Kjeldahl Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
