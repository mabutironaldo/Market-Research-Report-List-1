<p><p>Tire carbon black is categorized into several types. Insulating carbon black is used in tire sidewalls for insulation and heat resistance. Conductive carbon black enhances the tire's conductivity to prevent static electricity buildup. Water-soluble black is a type of carbon black that dissolves in water, commonly used in tire tread compounds. Easily dispersed carbon black is formulated for better dispersion in rubber, resulting in improved tire performance. Other types of tire carbon black include those with specialty properties, such as abrasion resistance or coloration.</p></p>
