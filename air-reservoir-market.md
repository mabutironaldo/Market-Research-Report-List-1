<p><p>Type I air reservoirs in the market typically refer to single-chambered reservoirs that store compressed air in a single container. These reservoirs are commonly used in applications with moderate air storage needs. On the other hand, Type II air reservoirs usually comprise multiple chambers, allowing for the storage of compressed air separately. These reservoirs are suitable for applications that require different levels of pressure regulation or when different air sources need to be stored separately.</p></p>
