<p><p>The industrial grade carbon fiber market can be classified into two types: regular-tow carbon fiber and large-tow carbon fiber. Regular-tow carbon fiber refers to fibers that have a small tow size, typically around 12,000 filaments per tow. These fibers are commonly used in industries such as aerospace, automotive, and sports equipment. On the other hand, large-tow carbon fiber refers to fibers with a larger tow size, typically around 50,000 filaments per tow. These fibers are mainly used in industries that require high strength and stiffness, such as wind energy and pressure vessels.</p></p>
