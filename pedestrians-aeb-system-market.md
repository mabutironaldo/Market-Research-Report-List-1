<p><p>Pedestrians AEB (Autonomous Emergency Braking) systems are designed to enhance safety on the roads by mitigating collisions with pedestrians. The market includes three types of systems: </p><p>1. Forward Collision Warning alerts drivers when there is a potential collision with a pedestrian ahead, reducing reaction time. </p><p>2. Dynamic Brake Support automatically applies additional braking force if the driver's response to the warning is inadequate, helping to prevent or lessen the impact of a collision. </p><p>3. Crash Imminent Braking takes over full control of the vehicle's braking system when a crash is imminent, reducing the severity of the impact or avoiding it altogether.</p></p>
