<p><p>The cardiac output monitoring market is divided into three types based on the devices used: invasive, minimally-invasive, and non-invasive. Invasive devices involve inserting a catheter or probe into a major blood vessel or heart chamber to directly measure cardiac output. Minimally-invasive devices include methods that provide indirect measurements using less invasive techniques, such as pulse contour analysis or bioimpedance. Non-invasive devices do not require any insertion and commonly utilize technologies like echocardiography or thoracic electrical bioimpedance to estimate cardiac output.</p></p>
