<p><p>The indirect drive turbine market consists of two main types: gas turbines and steam turbines. Gas turbines use natural gas or liquid fuels to produce energy through combustion, which drives a turbine. These turbines are commonly used for electricity generation, aircraft propulsion, and industrial processes. On the other hand, steam turbines use high-pressure steam to drive the turbine, which is typically generated by boilers fueled by coal, nuclear power, or natural gas. Steam turbines are widely used in power plants and other large-scale industrial applications.</p></p>
