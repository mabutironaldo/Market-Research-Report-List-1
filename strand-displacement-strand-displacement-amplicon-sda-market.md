<p><p>Strand Displacement Amplification (SDA) is a molecular biology technique that is used to amplify and detect specific DNA strands. It is a method that utilizes DNA polymerase to exponentially amplify target DNA sequences. The amplification process occurs through the displacement of one strand of the DNA duplex by a DNA polymerase enzyme, which then allows the newly synthesized complementary strand to act as a template for further amplification.</p><p>SDA offers several advantages over other nucleic acid amplification methods, such as PCR. It is highly specific, as it relies on the binding of specific primers to the target DNA sequence. It is also isothermal, meaning it operates at a constant temperature, which simplifies the overall process and reduces the need for sophisticated equipment. SDA is particularly useful in applications where a rapid and highly sensitive detection of specific DNA sequences is required, such as infectious disease diagnosis, genetic testing, and environmental monitoring.</p><p>The Strand Displacement Amplicon SDA market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
