<p><p>Molecular diagnostics systems refer to technologies used for analyzing and detecting genetic variations at a molecular level. There are several types of molecular diagnostics systems, including Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR), Hybridization, and DNA Sequencing. PCR amplifies specific DNA sequences, enabling identification and quantification of target genes. Hybridization involves the complementary binding of DNA or RNA fragments to identify specific sequences. DNA sequencing determines the order of nucleotides in a DNA molecule, providing detailed genetic information. These technologies are utilized in various fields, such as clinical and research settings, for disease diagnosis, genetic testing, and personalized medicine.</p></p>
