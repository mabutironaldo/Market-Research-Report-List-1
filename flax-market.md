<p><p>The flax market consists of three main types: flax fiber, flaxseed and flaxseed oil, and flax scraps. Flaxseed, on the other hand, is the seed of the flax plant, commonly consumed for its nutritional benefits and as a source of omega-3 fatty acids. Flaxseed oil is derived from pressing the seeds and is used in cooking and as a dietary supplement. Lastly, flax scraps refer to leftover parts after fiber extraction, often used as animal feed or in industrial applications.</p></p>
