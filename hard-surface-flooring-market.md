<p><p>Hard-surface flooring refers to flooring materials that are durable, non-resilient, and typically made of hard materials such as wood, ceramic, laminate, vinyl, or stone. One major driver for this market is the increasing demand from the construction industry, particularly in developing countries. The rapid urbanization, coupled with rising disposable incomes, has led to a surge in construction activities, thus driving the demand for hard-surface flooring.</p><p>Another factor contributing to the growth of the market is the rising preference for aesthetically pleasing and eco-friendly flooring options. Consumers are increasingly gravitating towards sustainable materials that offer a wide range of design possibilities. Additionally, the growing popularity of luxury vinyl tiles (LVT) and wood plastic composite (WPC) flooring is also fueling market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several key trends that are shaping its landscape. One such trend is the increasing adoption of digital printing technology, which allows manufacturers to replicate the look of natural materials such as wood or stone on hard-surface flooring products. This technology provides consumers with a cost-effective alternative to real materials.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising popularity of luxury vinyl tiles, which offer enhanced durability, water resistance, and ease of installation, is also a key trend in the market. Luxury vinyl tiles are available in a wide array of designs and patterns, replicating the look of natural materials, making them highly sought-after by consumers.</p><p>In conclusion, the hard-surface flooring market is poised for significant growth in the forecast period.
