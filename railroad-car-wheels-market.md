<p><p>The railroad car wheels market consists of two primary types: Monoblock Wheel and Strake Wheel. Monoblock wheels are made from a single piece of material, providing a strong and durable structure. They are commonly used in high-speed trains due to their stability and reliability. On the other hand, Strake wheels are designed with multiple pieces that are bolted together, offering flexibility in terms of maintenance and replacement. These wheels are typically used in freight trains where regular maintenance is required and cost-effectiveness is important.</p></p>
