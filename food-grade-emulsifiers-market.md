<p><p>Food grade emulsifiers are additives used in food products to stabilize and improve their texture and appearance. There are two types of emulsifiers: ionic emulsifiers and nonionic emulsifiers. Ionic emulsifiers contain charged molecules and are often used in products with low pH levels, such as dairy products. Nonionic emulsifiers, on the other hand, do not contain any charges and are commonly used in products with high pH levels, such as baked goods. Both types play a crucial role in creating stable and consistent food products.</p></p>
