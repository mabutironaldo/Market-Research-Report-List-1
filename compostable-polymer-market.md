<p><p>The compostable polymer market contains various types of polymers that are environmentally friendly and can be broken down into compost. Starch-based plastics are derived from renewable resources like corn or potato starch. Polylactic Acid (PLA) is made from corn or sugarcane and biodegrades into water and carbon dioxide. Polyhydroxy Alkanoates (PHA) are produced by bacteria and are fully biodegradable. Polyesters like PBS, PBAT, and PCL are derived from fossil fuels and degrade into water and carbon dioxide. Cellulose derivatives are made from wood pulp and can decompose naturally.</p></p>
