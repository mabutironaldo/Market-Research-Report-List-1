<p><p>A self-expanding metal stent (SEMS) is a medical device used in minimally invasive procedures to treat blockages in various organs, including the esophagus, bronchi, biliary tract, and urinary tract. It is typically made of nitinol, a shape memory alloy that enables the stent to be compressed for delivery and then self-expand once deployed. The stent structure provides support and keeps the affected organ's passageway open, improving the patient's ability to breathe, swallow, or drain fluids.</p><p>The global self-expanding metal stent market is expected to witness healthy growth over the forecast period. Factors driving market growth include the rising prevalence of diseases like cancer and gastrointestinal disorders, increasing preference for minimally invasive procedures, technological advancements in stent design, and a growing geriatric population. <p><p>The Self-Expanding Metal Stent (SEMS) market comprises three types: Fully, Partially, and Uncovered stents. A Fully Covered SEMS is designed to provide complete coverage of the affected area, ensuring the stent does not protrude. Partially Covered SEMS combines covered and uncovered sections, allowing fluids to pass while providing structural support. Uncovered SEMS, as the name suggests, has no covering, facilitating fluid drainage. These different types offer physicians versatility in selecting the appropriate stent based on the specific requirements of the patient's condition, ensuring optimal treatment outcomes.</p></p>
