<p><p>Biodegradable agricultural films are classified into different types based on their composition. Starch films are made primarily from starch extracted from various plants, while starch blended with polylactic acid (PLA) films are a combination of starch and a biodegradable polymer derived from renewable resources. Starch blended with polyhydroxyalkanoate (PHA) films are a blend of starch and a biodegradable polymer known for its excellent barrier properties. The "others" category includes agricultural films made from other biodegradable materials such as polycaprolactone (PCL) or polybutylene adipate terephthalate (PBAT). These films provide alternatives to traditional plastic films, reducing environmental impact.</p></p>
