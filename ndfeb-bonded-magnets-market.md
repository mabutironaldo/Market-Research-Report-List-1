<p><p>NdFeB bonded magnets are widely used in various industries due to their strong magnetic properties. There are two main types of manufacturing processes for these magnets: injection molding and pressing.</p><p>Injection molding involves the melting of NdFeB magnetic powders and then injecting them into a mold cavity to form the magnet. This process allows for complex shapes and high production rates.</p><p>Pressing, on the other hand, involves compacting the NdFeB powders into the desired shape using a hydraulic press. This method is suitable for producing magnets with simpler shapes and offers cost advantages for mass production.</p><p>Both injection molding and pressing are essential techniques for producing NdFeB bonded magnets, catering to different requirements of shape complexity and production volume.</p></p>
