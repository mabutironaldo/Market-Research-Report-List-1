<p><p>Impervious graphite is available in two main market types - powder and blocky. In the powder market, impervious graphite is finely ground into a powder form, making it suitable for various applications such as lubricants, coatings, and refractories. On the other hand, in the blocky market, impervious graphite is formed into solid blocks or shapes, offering superior thermal conductivity and resistance to chemical corrosion. Blocky impervious graphite is commonly used in industries like metallurgy, chemical processing, and energy, where it is applied in furnaces, crucibles, electrodes, and other high-temperature equipment.</p></p>
