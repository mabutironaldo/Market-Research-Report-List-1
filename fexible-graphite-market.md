<p><p>Flexible graphite, also known as expanded graphite, is a type of graphite material that has been treated to expand its volume. It is produced by intercalating sulfuric acid between graphite layers, then rapidly heating the material, causing the intercalated acid to vaporize and expand the graphite. This process results in a highly flexible and resilient material with excellent thermal conductivity, chemical resistance, and electrical conductivity properties.</p><p>The current outlook of the flexible graphite market is positive and is expected to experience steady growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for flexible graphite is driven by several factors. Firstly, its superior thermal conductivity makes it an ideal material for use in high-temperature applications, such as gaskets, seals, and heat exchangers. Additionally, its excellent chemical resistance makes it suitable for use in corrosive environments, such as in the chemical and petrochemical industries. Moreover, its flexibility allows it to be easily molded into various shapes, making it versatile for use in a wide range of applications.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing demand for energy-efficient solutions and the increasing focus on reducing emissions in various industries are expected to drive the demand for flexible graphite in the future.
